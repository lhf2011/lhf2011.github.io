LiDAR feature detection

2020-09-11 6 min read

feature detection: Identity the interest points.

Why not ICP

ICP(Iterative Closest Point) requires proper initial guess.
ICP requires a high overlapping ratio. With the increase of overlapping ratios, the convergence accuracy and speed of ICP algorithm improves.

Ideas from image -- Harris

Consider a patch in an Image, w(x,y) is the windows function, means the weight of points in the patch are >0( or 1 for simplify), for points outside the patch are 0.
In image, to get the gradient of the (x,y), we can make a small shift of the patch by [u,v], the intensity change is

Native in 3D geometry -- ISS(Intrinsic Shape Signatures)

ISS is a shape descriptor for 3D object recognition.
Keypoints are those have large variations in their neighborhood.
How to detect keypoints? PCA(principle component analysis) to get the smallest eigenvalue of the covariance matrix, if it's large, find a keypoint!
Eigenvalues of Cov(pi)Cov(p_{i})Cov(pi​) are λi1{\lambda_{i}}^1λi​1，λi2{\lambda_{i}}^2λi​2，λi3{\lambda_{i}}^3λi​3, in the decreasing order.
A flat surface can be λi1=λi2>λi3{\lambda_{i}}^1={\lambda_{i}}^2>{\lambda_{i}}^3λi​1=λi​2>λi​3.
A line can be λi1>λi2=λi3{\lambda_{i}}^1>{\lambda_{i}}^2={\lambda_{i}}^3λi​1>λi​2=λi​3.
Take a NMS(non-maximum suppression) to exclude similar eigenvalue, thus remove noise's effect, because we have to ensure λi1>λi2>λi3{\lambda_{i}}^1>{\lambda_{i}}^2>{\lambda_{i}}^3λi​1>λi​2>λi​3.
