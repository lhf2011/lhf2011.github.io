Rotation matrix

The most commonly used method, the homogeneous form fully describe the motion/transform(R+T), while inhomogeneous form only describe the rotation(R), not translation(T) included.

Disadvantages

Taking 9 numbers to describe 3 DOF, there is information redundancy.

Axis-Angle

It also called Rotation vector, Euler vector.
In this case, both the rotation axis and the angle are represented by a vector θ=θn\bm{ \theta} = \theta\bm{n}θ=θn, n\bm{n}n is the rotation axis and θ\thetaθ is the rotation angle.

Disadvantages

It's singularity.

Advantage

It's easy to understand, therefore usually used to display, not for calculation.

Disadvantages

Quaternion

It combines a real value and imagenary vector, and it's generally represented in this form: q=q0+q1i+q2j+q3k\bm{q}=q_{0} + q_{1}i + q_{2}j + q_{3}kq=q0​+q1​i+q2​j+q3​k
i, j ,k are imagenary part, they are the fundamental quaternion units.

Advantage

It's nonsingular, and no redundancy;
It's easy to take Interpolation. The slerp(spherical linear interpolation) and squad, they are usually used in interpolation between keyframes.
